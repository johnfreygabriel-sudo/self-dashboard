What Is It?

Not your average vending machine

A flower vending machine is a refrigerated, glass-front locker cabinet stocked with hand-arranged bouquets and individual stems. Each compartment has its own glass door so you can see exactly what you're buying.

When you pay, just that one door unlocks. You open it, take your flowers, and you're done. The whole thing takes under a minute — day or night, with no staff required.

🍕 Glass-front compartments ❄ Refrigerated inside 📲 Touchscreen + contactless pay 🎉 24-hour access ♾ No staff needed ♿ Wheeled & repositionable

Did You Know?

Interesting Facts

01

They started in Japan

Japan has had flower vending machines since the 1990s, particularly at train stations where commuters pick up flowers on the way home.

02

Flowers stay fresh longer than you think

The 4°C refrigerated compartments slow cell breakdown in cut flowers, often keeping them fresh for 5–7 days after purchase.

03

The busiest hours are late evening

Most flower vending machine sales happen between 6pm and 10pm — when traditional florists are closed but people still need flowers.

04

Hospitals are the top location

Hospital lobbies consistently see the highest flower machine sales. Visitors arriving at any hour can always bring something thoughtful.

05

Zero-waste restocking

Unsold flowers nearing the 48-hour mark are donated to care homes and charities before restocking, so nothing goes to waste.

06

The market is growing fast

The global flower vending machine market is expanding rapidly, driven by demand for 24/7 retail and the rise of automated convenience stores.

Common Questions

FAQ

Machines are restocked every 48 hours and kept at 4°C. Flowers are cut-to-order from local farms and loaded within 24 hours of cutting. Once purchased, they typically stay fresh for another 5–7 days with proper care.
You select a compartment on the central touchscreen, confirm the price, and pay by contactless card or phone. An electronic latch releases just that one door — it clicks open slightly. You open it fully, take your flowers, and close it again. The other compartments stay locked throughout.
All modern flower vending machines accept contactless debit and credit cards, Apple Pay, and Google Pay via NFC tap. Most do not accept cash. Some also support app-based payments and pre-ordering.
Most operators offer a simple refund process via the machine's app or a QR code on the receipt. Take a photo and submit within 24 hours for a full refund. The machines also flag damaged stock to operators remotely.
A typical glass-locker flower machine is around 180cm tall, 130cm wide, and 55cm deep. It runs from a standard plug socket and has lockable castors for easy repositioning indoors.
The machine operator (not the venue) handles all restocking and maintenance. Venues simply provide the floor space and a plug socket. Remote monitoring alerts the operator to any technical issues automatically.
